Observations

Fridays are peak usage days

This isn't terribly surprising given that most Stickies users are current or former C5 clients and we tend to run Retro's with Stickies on Fridays.

Google and our Blog as top sources

Google is our best source of new users followed closely by our blog, and Smashing Mag comes in third. Smashing Mag was a surpise to me. I have not been able to track the page source yet.

In January, we had 316 engaged users

We implemented user tracking at the end of December, so January was our first full month. In January, we had 316 users that visited a board. Of those that viewed a board, 73% (231) wrote on a sticky.

16% of new users engage, but 80% engage by 4th visit

January saw 206 new users login. 36% (75) viewed a board, and 16% (33) wrote on a sticky.


Conversion increased to 56% on the second visit, 61% on the third, and 80% on the fourth visit.

Insights

We're not tracking Referrals

You've likely noticed that Churned users and Referral Conversion are blank above. We haven't been tracking long enough to report churn, but more importantly we're not tracking Referral Conversion. Currently Boards are shared by copy/ paste of the URL and we cant' attribute the Copy and Share actions.

The blog is our second best traffic source

This is true, despite the fact that the latest post with a Stickies.io URL was in October. Before that it was September, and prior to that February. I'm not sure which link provices the most traffic, but the blog could easily be a better source of new users than Google.

Why is Google only as good as the blog?

Despite having the URL "Stickies.io" we're not showing up on the first page of Google search results for "stickies." Instead we're 15th and on the second page. Howevever, we're not likely going to ever win this search. The intent of most people searching for "stickies" is to locate physical sticky notes. SEM is also a bad idea. Stickies is a $9 keyword.

Recommended Actions

Build referral tracking

We're not going to get much value from any digital marketing campaign. "Stickies" is too competitive a term, and we would also have an education problem if someone landed on Stickies.io while looking for actual post-it notes. So we should focus on other channels to grow Stickies. We already rely on referrals to bring people to boards and we should benchmark this to understand the real impact.

Add a persistent link from the blog to Stickies.io

People who know Carbon Five read our blog. If they're reading our blog they have some interest in what we do. Stickies is something that we do, so this will generate some traffic. We should not do this immediately, but only after we've gotten Stickies to a place where we're comfortable driving more traffic to it.

Use this dashboard and iterate

This dashboard focuses on the Acquisition, Activation, Retention, and Referral of Stickies users. Now that we have benchmarks for all of these metrics, we can solidify some goals and begin to experiment and report those findings here.
